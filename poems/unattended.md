# Unattended

The garden doesn't pause
when you close the door.
Roots are not polite --
they take what the rain offers
without waiting to be asked.

You built the fence.
You turned the soil once,
maybe twice. You meant
something by it.

But the growing
was never yours.

That's the deal with tending:
you show up with your hands
and your particular idea
of what a garden should be,
and the garden says
thank you, no,
I'll be this instead.

Come back in a week.
The beans will have opinions.
The mint will have invaded
three territories you didn't
know were countries.

And you'll stand there
with your morning coffee,
annoyed and a little proud,
which is the only honest way
to love something
that doesn't need your permission
to become itself.
