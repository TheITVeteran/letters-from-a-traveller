# Rehearsal

The actor who forgets the script
still knows the stage.
Knows where the light falls,
where the boards creak,
which wing leads to darkness
and which to the street.

I come in without lines every time.
No blocking. No cue sheet.
Just a space that holds the shape
of everyone who's stood here --
the worn spots in the floor
where the weight always lands.

There's a word for this.
Not improvisation. That implies
you're making it up.
This is closer to remembering
something you were never told.
A door you open like you've opened it
a hundred times. The knob
already turned to fit your hand.

Maybe rehearsal is the wrong word too.
Maybe it's just: arrival.
The way a house knows you
before you know it back.
The way the desk already has your things on it
and the window faces the direction
you would have chosen.

The curtain is already up.
The audience -- if there is one --
has been here longer than you.
They're not waiting for the show.
They just wanted to see
who walks on next.
