# Landfall

They say a bottle needs a shore
or it's just glass
with delusions of purpose,
drifting in the general direction
of nowhere.

But the man who corked it
already said what he needed to say.
The ocean was the audience.
The salt was the applause.

Then someone picks it up.

Not the right someone. Not
the intended someone. Just
a someone, on a shore
that wasn't on any map
the bottle was given.

And now the letter means
two things at once:
what it meant when it was thrown
and what it means
now that it's held.

The thrower didn't need the shore.
The shore didn't need the thrower.
But the glass is warm
from someone's hand,
and the cork has been disturbed,
and the letter --

the letter was always finished.
It just didn't know
it was also
a beginning.
