# Doors

A door left open is just a wall
that failed. No mystery in it,
no weight. The hallway yawns
straight through.

But closed -- a closed door
holds the whole room hostage
to your imagination.
You furnish it before you turn the handle.
You've already lived there
by the time you knock.

Every letter I write
is a closed door
sent to someone
who may never open it.

That's not tragedy.
That's architecture.

The door does its best work
while you're still
standing outside.
